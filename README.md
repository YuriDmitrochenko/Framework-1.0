Overview

THE FRAMEWORK 1.0 is a conceptual monograph proposing a unified language for describing biological organization across multiple scales: from the living cell to tissues, organs, aging, regeneration, metabolic disease, neurodegeneration, and oncogenesis.

The central idea of the Framework is that biological states are determined not only by genetic and regulatory networks, but also by the physical and energetic conditions under which living systems operate. These include hydrogel organization, energy flux, metabolic resource availability, remodeling capacity, and structural constraints.

The work is presented as a first-generation conceptual and logical framework. It does not claim to be a completed mathematical theory. Many components are working hypotheses intended to generate testable predictions and future experimental programs.


Core Concepts

The Framework introduces and develops several linked concepts:

Living cell as an active hydrogel
Syneresis as a physical process of biological degradation
Basal Glycolytic Regime (BGR)
Energy Window
Constraint Architecture
Space of Permissible States
Regeneration Pendulum
Reverse Differentiation Scale
Local BGR Recycling Ratio (LBGRR)
Aging as progressive energetic insufficiency
Oncogenesis as access to alternative biological states

Structure of the Monograph

The monograph is organized into four major conceptual parts:

Chapter 1 — The Living Hydrogel

This chapter develops the physical foundation of the Framework. It interprets the cell as an energy-supported hydrogel-like system that continuously resists spontaneous degradation and syneresis.

Chapter 2 — Tissue Organization and Remodeling

This part extends the Framework from the individual cell to tissues, emphasizing remodeling, constraint restoration, regeneration, and energetic accessibility of biological states.

Chapter 3 — Higher Levels of Biological Organization

This chapter examines how cellular and tissue-level principles scale toward organs and the organism as a whole.

Chapter 4 — Biological Time

This part applies the Framework to aging, metabolic disease, neurodegeneration, oncogenesis, reproductive longevity, and systemic energetic decline.
